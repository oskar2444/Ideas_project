This repository houses the ZeroFOD Control project, developed in partnership with MTU Aero Engines, Germany's premier aircraft engine manufacturer, specifically focusing on their operations in Aviation Valley. Our primary mission is to develop innovative solutions for aviation safety and efficiency. Working as a self-organizing team that covers essential roles like Scrum Master, Developers, QA, and Analysts, we are collaborating with our Product Owner to deliver this solution through an Agile methodology featuring two-week sprints.The core challenge we are addressing is Foreign Object Debris, which refers to any particle, tool, or substance that does not belong in an engine assembly zone. Because FOD can cause catastrophic engine damage leading to massive financial losses and severe safety hazards, maintaining a strict "Zero FOD" environment is absolutely critical for flight safety. To achieve this, our software utilizes camera-based image analysis powered by AI and computer vision technology to provide real-time detection of foreign objects across various sizes and materials.Our system automatically classifies items such as metals, plastics, tools, components, and packaging. Once a hazard is detected, the application sends instant notifications to operators, supervisors, and maintenance teams. Beyond immediate alerts, the software performs comprehensive event logging that includes timestamps, images, and specific location markers. This logged data fuels advanced trend analytics designed to identify recurring FOD sources and high-risk zones within the facility.Ultimately, the project will culminate in three main deliverables. We will provide a comprehensive business analysis detailing the system's purpose, value proposition, and ROI projections. We will also deliver a functional, easy-to-run prototype using a Docker, simple script, or web-based implementation. Finally, our development journey, key decisions, and final results will be showcased in a compelling pitch presentation.
